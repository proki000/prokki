Types of Trees

Oak

The Oak tree is known for its strength and longevity. Found in many forests, it is a symbol of endurance and stability.

Maple

Maple trees are famous for their beautiful fall foliage and the sweet syrup made from their sap. They add vibrant color to any landscape.

Cherry Blossom

Cherry Blossoms are celebrated for their delicate beauty. They bloom in the spring, symbolizing renewal and the fleeting nature of life.

Pine

Pine trees are evergreen conifers known for their needle-like leaves and cones. They thrive in various climates and are often associated with winter and the holidays.

Birch

Birch trees are easily recognizable by their distinctive white bark. They are often found in temperate climates and are known for their light wood and ornamental appeal.

Willow

Willow trees are known for their long, sweeping branches that create a graceful, flowing appearance. They are often found near water sources and symbolize flexibility and resilience.

Redwood

Redwoods are among the tallest and oldest trees in the world, found primarily on the west coast of North America. They are renowned for their immense height and reddish-brown bark.
